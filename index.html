Who We Are

We’re sprinting to do our part to help our medical professionals amid the pandemic.

We are Mitochondrial - an upcoming product firm by a group of students from IIT Guwahati - and we are currently vying to bring intubation boxes to help our doctors fight the novel coronavirus.

Also called aerosol boxes, these are essential pieces of personnel protective equipment (PPEs) for our medical staff on the frontline and are currently short on supply in our fight against the Coronavirus Infectious Disease - 2019. Our discussions with doctors, medical students and our professors have established that a most effective way to deal with the ongoing shortage of PPE is to provide alternatives to obstruct the transfer of moisture from the patient to the staff, allowing the care professional to assist the patient with minimal risk. Taking advantage of the open-source design by Dr. Hsien Yung Lai from Taiwan, we’ve re-worked the box for the present context: making it easy to manufacture amid the national lockdown and simpler still to assemble while keeping it cost-effective, so that our heroes may continue to serve, unnerved.

We aim to build and supply these boxes to government hospitals, free of cost. Please help us serve as much point-of-care medical staff, as possible. They are our only line of defence against the SARS-CoV-2. Let's come forward to save them, for them to save us.

Design

Ours is an assembly optimized version of the open-source design by Dr. Hsien Yung Lai, making the low-cost and reusable PPE even more cost-effective and more efficient for manufacturing, storage and distribution. We’ve made it such that one may assemble it themselves, on-site. These (modifications) shall prove crucial in protecting our medical staff using minimum resources amid a nationwide lockdown.

Prototyping & Feedback

Through a rigorous design process involving subject matter experts such as Dr. Johann Christoper (MD, DNB, Care Hospitals), Dr. Abhijeet Bhati (ENT, NEIGRIHMS), Prof. S. Kanagaraj (Biomedical Systems, Department of Mechanical Engineering, IIT Guwahati), Dr. Sajan. Manufacturing & Delivery

Their ease of manufacturing implies the ability to produce them anywhere in the country and their ease of transportation and storage implies the ability to deliver these well within schedule. Their ease of assembly implies that we deliver them boxed like pizzas, seasoned with zip-ties, along with the heartfelt letter you’ve penned, for a nurse to open and be good to go, save a life while protecting her own.
